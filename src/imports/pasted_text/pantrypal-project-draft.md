Project Overview:
PantryPal is a grocery tracking and simple meal planning app that helps users track food they already have, reduce food waste, create grocery lists, and find simple meal ideas based on pantry items. The primary platform is mobile iOS/Android. The secondary platform is web.

Pitch Statement:
“PantryPal is a simple meal-planning app on mobile and web that helps users track food they already have, create grocery lists, and find easy meal ideas using an organized checklist system that reduces food waste, saves money, and makes grocery planning less stressful.”

Target Audience:
PantryPal is made for busy people who need a simple way to manage food at home. The target audience includes college students on tight budgets, single working adults who get home tired after work, and families responsible for planning meals for multiple people.

This is an important user need because grocery planning takes attention that many people do not always have. A college student may be focused on classes, homework, and part-time work. A single adult may be tired after work, the gym, or hobbies and may not want to figure out dinner from scratch. A parent may be responsible for lunches, snacks, dinners, and household basics for the whole family. PantryPal helps reduce that pressure by making food tracking and meal planning easier to manage.

Target Platforms:
Primary Platform: Mobile app for iOS and Android
Secondary Platform: Web app

Platform Reasoning:
The mobile app is the best primary platform because PantryPal is most useful while users are shopping, putting groceries away, or checking food quickly from the kitchen. Users can update items right after buying groceries, check their list while walking through a store, and mark food as used without needing a computer. The web app is a useful secondary platform for bigger planning tasks like organizing a full grocery list, reviewing meal ideas, or managing a family pantry list.

Design Rationale:
Use this text:
For PantryPal’s design system, we chose Inter as the primary typeface because it is clean, readable, and works well on mobile screens. PantryPal users may be checking groceries while shopping, cooking, or unpacking food, so the typography needs to be easy to scan quickly. Inter also works for both headings and body text, which keeps the interface consistent and simple. The type scale uses a 1.25 ratio starting from a 16px base size. This gives the app enough hierarchy for headings, cards, labels, and body text without making the design feel too dramatic or crowded.

The color palette uses an analogous and natural color harmony based around greens, creams, and warm food-related accents. Pantry Green is the main brand color because it connects to freshness, groceries, and organization. Warm Cream and Soft Sage create a calm background that feels less harsh than plain white. Tomato Red is used carefully for expiration warnings, Lemon Yellow is used for low-stock alerts, and Sky Blue is used for links or helpful information.

For accessibility, the design should avoid using color alone to communicate important states. Expiration warnings and low-stock alerts should include icons, labels, or text along with color. Text should have strong contrast against backgrounds, especially on buttons and alert cards. Since PantryPal is mobile-first, buttons should be large enough to tap easily, and body text should stay at least 16px for readability.
